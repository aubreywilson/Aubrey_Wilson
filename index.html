Aubrey Wilson's How to Conduct A Journalism Interview

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to interview anyone

Assembly time: 60 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Have you ever wanted to know someone's story? Interviews are an effective way to share experiences that can provide information that can not be taught by other methods.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Camera
  • Microphone
  • Tripod
  • Prepared Questions
  • A story

  1. Research your subject.

    Before the interview: read articles, check socials, and learn their background so you can make informed questions.

  2. Prepare open-ended quesitons.

    Encourage detailed answers, focusing on "how" and "why" instead of yes or no answers.

  3. Set up interview professionally.

    Contact your source clearly, explaining your purpose, setting up a time, place, or platform.

  4. Ask follow-up questions.

    Based on responses, ask follow-up questions to get deeper insights and more meaningful quotes for your story.

  5. Share the story.

    After editing the interview, share your story on any platform or social media you would like.

  6. Review your notes or recordings.

    After the interview, thank your sources, following up if you need any clarification.

    A real-world interview that would be shared on the news.
